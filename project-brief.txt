17: AI Rapid Sorting of Underwater Ecological Imagery
Subject Matter Expert: Joshua Riddell from FFT, josh@fft.ai
Key Topics / Themes: Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, Image Processing, Object Detection,
Semantic Search
Problem Space
Marine ecological monitoring relies heavily on large-scale underwater image collection to assess
biodiversity, species distribution, and ecosystem health. FFT has developed a technology for underwater
ecological assessment - towed imaging device that “flies” through the water behind a boat, capturing up
to 50 km of transect imagery per day for ecological assessment.
While data collection is now highly efficient, the bottleneck lies in post-processing. Thousands of
underwater frames must be manually reviewed, labelled, and categorised to identify marine species and
ecological features. This process is time-consuming, labour-intensive, and limits the speed at which
ecological insights can be generated.
Additionally, underwater imagery presents significant challenges for computer vision: low visibility,
colour distortion, motion blur, occlusion, and high object variability. Although object detection models
exist, their performance often depends on high-quality labels and domain-specific adaptation. There is
an opportunity to significantly accelerate ecological analysis by developing intelligent tools that assist,
rather than replace, human experts.
Project Brief
This project explores how artificial intelligence and human-in-the-loop interface design can accelerate
the sorting and labelling of underwater ecological imagery.
Students will design and prototype a digital system that improve annotation efficiency and enhance
object detection model performance, with the focus not only on model accuracy, but also on workflow
optimisation - creating a tool that meaningfully reduce manual labelling time while improving detection
quality in a challenging underwater domain.
Enhancements such as conversational querying, active learning workflows, or automated dataset
curation may be explored as extensions.
Project Requirements
• Advanced vision models (e.g., DINOv3, SAM, or similar foundation models) to support generalised
object detection, segmentation, and semantic search across underwater datasets.
• Include intelligent grouping or tracking mechanisms to identify recurring objects across adjacent
frames, and enabling intuitive dataset exploration.
• Enable semantic search or filtering of images based on object characteristics or detected features.
• Provide an intuitive user interface that allows researchers to review, validate, and refine model
outputs.
